## 1. UK Energy Market Data

### Ofgem Price Cap (2026 reference)
- The Ofgem price cap is reviewed quarterly (Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct).
- Typical household annual bill (dual fuel, direct debit): **~£1,738/yr** (Q1 2026 benchmark — update each quarter).
- Unit rates (typical): electricity ~24–28p/kWh, gas ~6–7p/kWh.
- Standing charges: electricity ~61p/day, gas ~31p/day.

### Supplier Landscape
Major suppliers active in the UK comparison market:
- British Gas, EDF, E.ON Next, Octopus Energy, Ovo Energy, Scottish Power, Shell Energy, Utilita, Bulb (now Octopus).
- Octopus Energy and EDF are currently the highest-converting suppliers on comparison platforms.

### Average Savings
- Switching from SVT (Standard Variable Tariff) to a fixed deal: **£200–£480/yr** depending on usage and region.
- Average saving displayed on Caldelo: **£480/yr** for energy category.

### Tariff Types
- Fixed rate — price locked for 12–24 months, early exit fees apply.
- Variable / SVT — follows price cap, no exit fees.
- Time-of-use (e.g. Octopus Go) — cheaper overnight rates, ideal for EV owners.
- Green / renewable — 100% renewable sourced electricity.

## 2. EV Charging Data

### UK EV Market Context
- UK EV registrations: ~16% of new car sales (2025).
- Over 60,000 public charge points across the UK (ZapMap, 2025).
- Government target: all new cars zero emission by 2035.

### Home Charging
- Average home charger: 7kW smart charger.
- Installation cost: £800–£1,200 (post-OZEV grant where applicable).
- OZEV grant: up to £350 off a home charger for eligible renters/flat owners.
- Overnight charging cost on standard tariff: ~£5–£9 per full charge (average EV ~60kWh battery).
- Best EV tariffs (e.g. Octopus Go, OVO Drive): electricity from ~7–9p/kWh overnight vs 24–28p standard.
- Annual saving on dedicated EV tariff vs standard: **£200–£400/yr** depending on mileage.
- Average saving displayed on Caldelo: **£320/yr** for EV Charging category.

### Public Charging Networks
Key networks: Pod Point, BP Pulse, Osprey, Gridserve, Osprey, Char.gy, ubitricity (lamp posts).
- Rapid (50kW): ~65–85p/kWh
- Ultra-rapid (150kW+): ~75–99p/kWh
- Slow/fast (7–22kW): ~45–60p/kWh or free (some supermarkets/retail)

## 3. Solar & Battery Data

### UK Solar Market Context
- Average solar installation: 4kWp system (10–12 panels).
- Average installation cost: **£6,000–£9,000** (4kWp, 2025 market rate).
- Average annual generation: 3,400–4,200 kWh/yr (UK average, south-facing roof).
- Payback period: 7–12 years depending on usage and export tariff.
- Government support: no VAT on solar installations (0% VAT since 2023).

### Export Tariffs (Smart Export Guarantee — SEG)
- Suppliers must offer SEG if they have >150,000 customers.
- Rates: 4–24p/kWh depending on supplier and tariff (Octopus Agile export can exceed this).
- Best current SEG rates: Octopus Energy (~15p), EDF (~12p), E.ON Next (~12p).

### Battery Storage
- Average battery (e.g. Tesla Powerwall 3, GivEnergy): 10–15kWh capacity.
- Cost: £4,000–£8,000 installed.
- Combined solar + battery saving: **£700–£1,200/yr** vs no solar.

### Average Savings (Caldelo display)
- Solar category saving to be confirmed — do not display until calculator supports solar inputs.

## 5. Approved Copy Rules

### Must Always Use
- "UK households" not "British households" or "customers"
- "switch" not "sign up" or "move" when referring to tariff changes
- "comparison" not "broker" — Caldelo is a comparison service
- "tariff" not "plan" or "package" for energy products
- "£" symbol before all savings figures
- "/yr" after annual savings (not "per year" or "p.a.")
- "Start saving" or "See my savings" as primary CTA copy — no variations without approval
- "No credit check required" in trust signals — this is a key differentiator

### Must Never Use
- "cheapest" as an absolute claim (use "better deal" or "lower tariff")
- "guaranteed savings" — savings depend on usage
- "we recommend" — Caldelo compares, it does not recommend
- "sign up" for energy switching (implies a new account, not a switch)
- American English: "color", "center", "license" (as noun for energy), "optimize"

### Tone Principles
- Plain English. No jargon.
- On the user's side, not the providers'.
- Confident but not pushy.
- Honest about how the business works (referral fees, not magic).
